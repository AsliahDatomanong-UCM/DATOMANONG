Ramadhan Around the World

Ramadhan unites Muslims globally through fasting, prayer, and charity, yet local customs create vibrant diversity in its observance.

Middle East

UAE elevates iftar with opulent hotel tents offering global buffets alongside Emirati dates and luqaimat dumplings, capped by dazzling fireworks over Dubai's skyline, merging ancient rituals with contemporary luxury to draw locals and tourists alike.

Southeast Asia

Southeast Asia's Ramadan traditions beautifully blend local customs with Islamic devotion. In Indonesia, communities in sarongs gather 1-2 days before Ramadan for padusan rituals at sacred springs, rivers or beaches, taking flower-infused baths to purify body and soul, merging Javanese Hindu-Buddhist heritage with faith preparation.‚Äã

Malaysia's street bazaars overflow with rendang beef curry, charitable bubur lambuk porridge, and colorful kuih sweets like onde-onde, drawing crowds for affordable iftar bonding. Brunei signals iftar with historic cannon salutes from rooftops, followed by ambuyat sago feasts and royal Taraweeh prayers; Singapore's pasar malam markets offer sup sahur soups and roti john sandwiches for pre-dawn socializing, while Philippines' Moro communities host mass iftars with halal roasted chicken or beef in lechon style, echoed by village sahur calls amid tropical hospitality.

South Asia

Pakistan's chaand raat, the moon-sighting eve of Eid, transforms cities like Lahore and Karachi into vibrant hubs where markets overflow late into the night with women applying intricate henna (mehndi) designs, shopping for shimmering bangles, jewelry, and Eid outfits, while families savor sheer khurma‚Äîa creamy vermicelli pudding laced with milk, dates, nuts, and cardamom‚Äîas fireworks light the sky and mosques announce the new moon.

India's Bohri Muslims in Mumbai exemplify generosity through community trusts like the Anjuman-I-Islam, organizing massive thali feasts with biryani, sheer khurma, and kebabs distributed to thousands of needy families via door-to-door deliveries and langars, fostering unity in the bustling Bohri mohallas. Bangladesh ramps up zakat collections through mosque drives and NGOs, with rooftop meriam cannons booming at sunset to signal iftar across Dhaka, followed by streets filled with fuchka snacks, beef bhuna, and family gatherings under festive lights.
