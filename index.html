Ramadhan Kareem

A simple guide to understanding Ramadhan, why Muslims fast, and how to make the most of this sacred month.

Know the basics

What is Ramadhan?

Ramadhan is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar, observed as a time of fasting, prayer, reflection, and charity. It commemorates the revelation of the Qur’an and encourages discipline, empathy, and spiritual growth.

Why Do Muslims Fast?

Fasting is an act of worship and obedience to Allah. It teaches patience, humility, and compassion for those in need.

A Day During Ramadhan

A typical day begins with Suhoor (pre-dawn meal) and ends with Iftar (breaking the fast at sunset).

Do’s During Ramadhan

  • Begin the day with Suhoor
  • Break the fast with Iftar
  • Pray regularly and read the Qur’an
  • Give charity and help others
  • Practice patience and kindness

Don’ts During Ramadhan

  • Do not eat or drink during daylight
  • Avoid anger, gossip, and lying
  • Refrain from smoking
  • Avoid unnecessary conflicts
  • Do not neglect health or prayers
