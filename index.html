Ramadhan Kareem

A simple guide to understanding Ramadan, why Muslims fast, and how to make the most of this special month.

Start with the basics

What is Ramadhan?

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar, observed by Muslims worldwide as a holy month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community. It commemorates the month during which the Qur'an was revealed to the Prophet Muhammad. This sacred period is marked by spiritual growth and self-discipline. During Ramadan, Muslims fast from dawn until sunset, abstaining from food, drink, and other specific physical needs. Fasting cultivates mindfulness, patience, and empathy for those less fortunate. In addition to fasting, Muslims increase their prayers, charity, and reading of the Qur'an.

Do's during Ramadhan:

  • Begin each day with a pre-dawn meal called Suhoor.
  • Break your fast at sunset with Iftar, often shared with family and community.
  • Perform the five daily prayers punctually.
  • Engage in extra prayers and Quran recitation, especially the Taraweeh prayers at night.
  • Give to charity to help those in need.
  • Practice kindness, patience, and forgiveness.

Don'ts during Ramadhan:

  • Do not eat or drink during daylight hours.
  • Avoid negative behavior such as lying, gossiping, or anger.
  • Refrain from smoking and other harmful habits.
  • Avoid unnecessary arguments or conflicts.
  • Do not neglect your physical and spiritual health.
